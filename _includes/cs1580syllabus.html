You learn about the basic principles of C++ in CS 1570 and then you come to CS 1580 to get additional practice.

Attendance

Unless excused, missing a lab session will result in a grade of 0 for that day's assignment. Excused absences must be verified prior to the start of the class - email is recommended. Three or more unexcused absences will result in being dropped from the class.

Assignments

Each assignment will be graded as follows:

Correctness Compilation Style
Definition Your code performs the appropriate operations to achieve the intended results. Your code compiles successfully without warnings. Your code adheres to the established style guidelines .
Weight 40% 30% 30%
How to Avoid Carefully follow the instructions in the assignment; don't be afraid to ask questions; attend your CS 1570 class. Write your code incrementally by compiling often. Read through your code once you're done; have another person in lab (who has time) review your code.

Grades will be posted to Blackboard. Specific submission and compilation instructions will be provided with each assignment.

Warning!

Each assignment must be completed before the end of the lab session unless otherwise specified.

Failing to submit your assignment properly will result in a 20 point reduction (20% of total points possible) of that assignment's final grade.

Academic Dishonesty

Every student enrolled in this course is expected to be familiar with, and adhere to, Missouri S&T's Student Academic Regulations. Academic Dishonesty such as cheating, plagiarism, and sabotage will typically result in zero grades for the respective assignment, and notifications to the student's advisor, department chair, and the campus undergraduate studies office. Further academic sanctions may be imposed as well in accordance with the regulations.

Disabilities & Accessibility

If you have a documented disability and anticipate needing accommodations in this course, you are strongly encouraged to meet with the instructor as early as possible in the semester. You will need to request that the Disability Support Services staff send a letter to the instructor verifying your disability and specifying the accommodation you will need before the instructor can arrange your accommodation.

Visibility
Sometimes it can be hard to see things inside the PuTTY terminal because of the color scheme. If you're having some trouble, following these steps may help:
  1. Open PuTTY and connect to a session (enter your credentials if or when asked)
  2. Right-click on PuTTY logo in the upper left-hand corner of the window
  3. Select Change Settings...
  4. Under the Window category, select Colours
  5. Check the box User system colours
  6. Click Apply at the bottom of the window
  7. Done
